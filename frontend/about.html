Our Story

Built On A Simple Idea:
Guests Remember Feelings

Grand Dima didn't start as a hotel. It started as a small family kitchen that refused to serve anything it wouldn't serve its own guests at home — and a belief that hospitality is a craft, not a transaction.

Years later, that same kitchen sits at the heart of a hotel where every room is arranged the way you'd arrange your own home, if your home had a five-star chef and a view.

How It Began

From One Table
To A Hundred

The first "Grand Dima" was three tables and a wood-fired oven, run by a family that believed a good meal could turn strangers into regulars, and regulars into friends.

As word spread, so did the ambition — first a dining room, then a handful of rooms upstairs for travelers who didn't want the night to end. Today, that same instinct shapes every corner of the hotel: nothing is added unless it makes a guest's stay a little warmer.

Today

A Hotel That Still
Feels Like Family

Every staff member at Grand Dima is trained on one rule above all others: notice what a guest needs before they have to ask for it. Fresh towels, a quiet table, a room with the morning light — small things, done consistently, are what luxury actually is.

We're proud that many of our guests return not for the marble floors, but for the person at the front desk who remembers their name.

Looking Ahead

Our Goal Is To Become The Hotel Guests Compare Every Other Stay To

We're not chasing more rooms — we're chasing better ones. Over the next few years, Grand Dima is investing in sustainable energy for every suite, an expanded rooftop dining experience, and a scholarship kitchen program that trains the next generation of local chefs. Growth, to us, means raising the bar we hold ourselves to — not just the number of keys we hand out.

Sustainability

Moving every room to renewable energy and locally-sourced ingredients by design, not as an afterthought.

Community

A kitchen training program for young local chefs, so Grand Dima's standards live beyond our walls.

Craft

Never scaling faster than we can maintain the personal, remembered-your-name kind of service.
